Garland Water Rates (2025)

Garland uses tiered summer pricing, so a pool fill can get expensive:

  • Base: $16.40/month (includes first 2,000 gallons)
  • Next 8,000 gallons: $3.92 per 1,000 gallons
  • Next 15,000 gallons: $5.27 per 1,000 gallons
  • Over 25,000 gallons: $6.57 per 1,000 gallons

Example: Filling a 15,000-gallon pool in summer costs approximately $74 in water charges.

The good news about Garland water: it's generally well-balanced chemically, so you won't be fighting extreme pH or hardness issues like some other Texas cities.

Garland's Time-Tested Water System: As one of the older established cities in the DFW area, Garland has decades of experience optimizing its water treatment processes. The city's water system draws from proven sources and uses traditional treatment methods that have been refined over time to produce consistently balanced water. Garland water typically maintains pH levels between 7.6-8.0 with moderate hardness that requires minimal adjustment for pool startup.

The city's commitment to infrastructure maintenance is evident in its systematic replacement of aging water mains and continuous system upgrades. This proactive approach prevents the water quality fluctuations that can occur in systems under stress. Garland pool owners benefit from reliable water pressure and consistent chemistry that makes routine maintenance more predictable and cost-effective.
